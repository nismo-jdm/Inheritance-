🌙 Welcome to Islamic Inheritance (Ilm al-Faraid)

The science of Islamic inheritance (Faraid) is a noble branch of Islamic knowledge that ensures the fair and just distribution of wealth according to the divine wisdom of Allah (ﷻ) as revealed in the Qur'an and taught by Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).

يُوصِيكُمُ ٱللَّهُ فِىٓ أَوْلَـٰدِكُمْ ۖ لِلذَّكَرِ مِثْلُ حَظِّ ٱلْأُنثَيَيْنِ
"Allah commands you regarding your children: the share of the male is like that of two females."
— Surah An-Nisa (4:11)
وَلَكُمْ نِصْفُ مَا تَرَكَ أَزْوَٰجُكُمْ إِن لَّمْ يَكُن لَّهُنَّ وَلَدٌ
"And for you is half of what your wives leave if they have no child. But if they have a child, for you is one fourth of what they leave..."
— Surah An-Nisa (4:12)

📖 Foundations of Faraid

1. Order of Distribution

  1. Funeral expenses - First priority
  2. Debts - Must be paid before distribution
  3. Wasiyyah (Will) - Maximum 1/3 of estate, to non-heirs only
  4. Inheritance distribution - According to fixed shares

2. Categories of Heirs

  • Ashab al-Furud (Fixed Share Heirs) - Those with specified shares in Qur'an
  • Asabah (Residuaries) - Those who take remainder after fixed shares
  • Dhawil Arham (Distant Kindred) - Relatives who inherit when no closer heirs exist

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What if the deceased has only daughters and no sons? ▼

If there is one daughter, she receives 1/2 of the estate. If there are two or more daughters, they collectively receive 2/3 of the estate. The remainder goes to the closest male relative (Asabah) such as the father, grandfather, or brothers.

Evidence: "But if they are (only) daughters, two or more, theirs is two-thirds of the inheritance; and if there is only one, her share is half." (Surah An-Nisa 4:11)

What happens if the deceased has no children? ▼

The inheritance goes to other eligible heirs in the following order:

  • Spouse receives their fixed share (husband: 1/2, wife: 1/4)
  • Parents receive their shares
  • Siblings may inherit as Asabah or receive fixed shares
  • More distant relatives if no closer heirs exist
What is the 1/3 Wasiyyah (Will) rule? ▼

A Muslim can make a will (Wasiyyah) for up to one-third (1/3) of their estate after debts are paid. This portion can be given to non-heirs, charities, or distant relatives who don't inherit.

Important: The will cannot be made in favor of legal heirs who already have fixed shares, as this would upset the divinely ordained distribution.

Evidence: The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Allah has given everyone who has rights their due rights, so there is no will for an heir." (Sunan Abu Dawud 2870)

How does Sunni inheritance differ from other schools? ▼

This portal follows Sunni jurisprudence based on the four major schools (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali) which have minor differences in some details but agree on fundamental principles.

The main differences with other Islamic schools relate to:

  • Order of priority for certain distant relatives
  • Inheritance rights of specific family members
  • Application of Awl and Radd principles

This calculator follows mainstream Sunni positions based on Quranic verses and authentic Hadith.

What is Hajb (blocking/exclusion)? ▼

Hajb refers to the exclusion or reduction of an heir's share due to the presence of a closer heir. There are two types:

  • Hajb Hirman (Complete Exclusion): The heir receives nothing due to a closer relative (e.g., grandparents are excluded if parents exist)
  • Hajb Nuqsan (Reduction): The heir's share is reduced (e.g., mother receives 1/6 instead of 1/3 if there are children)
What are Awl and Radd? ▼

Awl (Proportional Reduction): When the total fixed shares exceed the estate, all shares are proportionally reduced. This was established by the Companions, particularly Umar ibn al-Khattab (رضي الله عنه).

Radd (Return/Increase): When fixed shares don't exhaust the estate and there are no Asabah (residuaries), the remainder is distributed proportionally among the fixed-share heirs (excluding the spouse in most schools).

Must debts be paid before inheritance? ▼

Yes, absolutely. The order of priority is:

  1. Funeral expenses
  2. Settlement of debts
  3. Execution of valid Wasiyyah (up to 1/3)
  4. Distribution of inheritance

Evidence: Allah says "after any bequest they may have made or debt" (Surah An-Nisa 4:11-12), placing debt settlement before inheritance.
