The Nutella Story

Born from
Scarcity,
Crafted for
the World

In 1946, postwar Italy faced a chocolate shortage. Pastry maker Pietro Ferrero created "Pasta Gianduja" — a brilliant blend of hazelnuts and the little available cocoa. What began as necessity became the world's most beloved spread.

Today, every jar of Nutella still carries that original spirit: simple, premium ingredients crafted with Italian artistry and an unwavering commitment to pure, unadulterated joy.

Brand Heritage

A Journey of Joy

1946
The Original Paste
Pietro Ferrero creates "Pasta Gianduja" in Alba, Italy — a cocoa-hazelnut block born from postwar cocoa rationing and Italian ingenuity.
1964
Nutella is Born
Michele Ferrero transforms the recipe into the iconic spreadable jar we know today. The name "Nutella" is trademarked and the world changes forever.
1969
Going Global
Nutella launches in France and Germany, beginning its unstoppable journey across Europe and into breakfast tables worldwide.
2007
World Nutella Day
The first World Nutella Day is celebrated on February 5th — a fan-created holiday that Ferrero officially adopted in 2015, uniting millions of fans globally.
Today
Spreading Happiness
Over 365,000 tons produced annually, enjoyed in 160+ countries. Nutella remains the world's favorite spread and a symbol of joy across generations.
